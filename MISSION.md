# MRCEM Primary Exam Preparation — Mission

## The Goal
Pass the MRCEM Primary exam on **27 October 2026** with a strong score. Not scrape by — pass with flying colours.

## Why This Matters
This is the first of three MRCEM exams required for the CESR (Certificate of Eligibility for Specialist Registration) pathway. Faz is an ST3+ Emergency Medicine clinician in a non-training post at Barts Health NHS Trust, targeting the GMC Specialist Register for Emergency Medicine. MRCEM Primary is the mandatory gateway — without it, neither SBA/OSCE nor FR CEM is accessible.

## Larger Context
- **CESR application** → Specialist Register → career progression
- **UK visa → ILR ~2028** — completing exams strengthens immigration case
- MRCEM is required for both CCT and CESR routes

## Exam Structure
- **Format:** 180 Single Best Answer (SBA) questions
- **Duration:** 3 hours
- **Content:** RCEM Basic Sciences Curriculum (June 2010)
- **Categories:** Anatomy (237 notes), Physiology (175), Pharmacology (140), Microbiology (109), Pathology (427), Evidence Based Medicine (31), Embryology (7)

## Key Dates
- **Application opens:** 15 July 2026
- **Exam date:** 27 October 2026
- **Prep window:** ~18 weeks from now (20 June → 27 Oct)
- **Notes platform:** `~/mrcem` — 1,126 study notes across all 7 categories

## Success Criteria
1. Pass the exam (RCEM does not publish the pass mark, but historically 60-65% is the threshold)
2. Achieve confidence across ALL 7 curriculum domains, not just clinical topics
3. Build long-term knowledge that carries into MRCEM SBA and clinical practice
4. Have a preparation system reusable for MRCEM SBA
