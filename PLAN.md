## Strategy

**Blueprint-weighted, interleaved blocks.** Anatomy (33%) and Physiology (33%) drive the schedule — they account for 120/180 questions. Every week touches at least one anatomy subcategory. Pathology and Microbiology are foundation content that gets covered early (Week 1-2) then reinforced through system-based weeks.

## Weekly Structure
- 4 structured lessons per week (Mon-Thu)
- 1 review/quiz day (Fri) — RCEMLearning Primary Practice modules
- 1 rest day (Sat or Sun)
- 1 flexible catch-up day
- Target: 15-20 hours/week

## Exam Reality Check
- **Pass rate:** ~25% (3/4 fail)
- **Pass mark:** ~108/180 (~60%) using Angoff method
- **Question style:** Clinical vignettes testing basic science knowledge. NOT simple recall. Nuanced SBAs with "all EXCEPT" common.
- **Anatomy detail:** Expect "outermost layer of stomach" (serosa), nerve distributions, muscle attachments
- **Physiology detail:** Mechanisms, not just facts. "Why does X happen when Y fails?"
- **Question banks alone insufficient** — need deep understanding per RCEM guidance
- **No negative marking** — answer every question
