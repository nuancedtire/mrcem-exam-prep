Leukocyte Migration — The Sequence

  1. Margination — leukocytes move to vessel wall
  2. Rolling — selectin-mediated (E-selectin, P-selectin)
  3. Adhesion — integrin-mediated (ICAM-1, VCAM-1)
  4. Diapedesis — transmigration through endothelium (PECAM-1)
  5. Chemotaxis — movement towards chemical gradient (LTB₄, C5a, IL-8)

Granulomatous Inflammation

  • Epithelioid macrophages + multinucleated giant cells + lymphocytes
  • Causes: TB, sarcoidosis, Crohn's, fungal, leprosy, foreign body, syphilis
  • Key: Granulomas form when the immune system can't clear the pathogen → walls it off
